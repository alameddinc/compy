Notes that travel with the web

Reading and researching online means juggling dozens of tabs. Here is how to capture what matters without losing the context it came from.

Alameddin Çelik · July 6, 2026

Most note-taking tools ask you to leave the page, switch to another app, and paste a link you will never click again. The context evaporates. A week later you have a note that says "fix this" and no idea what "this" was.

There is a simpler model. Highlight anything on the page and a note travels with it. When you return, the highlight is exactly where you left it, in the color you chose, with your comment one click away.

Your data stays yours

Privacy should be the default, not a paid upgrade. Notes stay on your device — nothing is uploaded to a server. There is no account to create and no sync you did not ask for. When you want a backup, you export an encrypted file that only your passphrase can open.

Tip: Every highlight can hold multiple comments, like a thread. Capture the first thought immediately, then add detail as you learn more.

Built for handing off to an AI

The best part shows up when the reading is done. Export your notes as a Markdown task list for your AI, drop it into a chat, and let the model work through a page of fixes with full context — the quote, the page, and the element each note points to.

It works the other way too. When you are the one doing the work, your highlights become a checklist you can walk top to bottom, across every site you touched this week.

Fast enough to stay out of your way

  • Press Alt+Shift+H to highlight the current selection.
  • Press Alt+Shift+N to highlight and add a note in one move.
  • Open the dashboard to see everything you saved, grouped by site.

Good tools disappear into the task. You should be thinking about the work, not about the tool that captures it. That is the whole idea: highlight, note, move on — and trust that it will all be waiting for you later.
